Top 10 Things to Do in the Smokies as Newlyweds

You just said "I do" in one of the most beautiful places on earth. Now what? We've lived here for decades — here's how to spend those first days as husband and wife.

By Zach & Bridgett

The Smoky Mountains have a way of making everything feel a little more sacred. The air is different up here. The views stop you mid-sentence. And when you're newly married, surrounded by ancient ridgelines and rushing streams, the whole world seems to slow down just enough to let it all sink in.

We've been lucky enough to call Sevier County home for a long time, and we've watched hundreds of couples discover it for the first time. Here are our ten favorites — the places and experiences we genuinely recommend when couples ask us, "So what should we actually do?"

"The mountains have a way of reminding you that some things are worth slowing down for."

  1. 01
    Watch Sunrise from Clingmans Dome At 6,643 feet, this is the highest point in the entire Smokies — and the highest point on the Appalachian Trail. Set your alarm, drive up before first light, and watch the world turn gold from above the clouds. It is genuinely one of the most extraordinary views in America, and you'll have it almost entirely to yourselves that early in the morning.
  2. 02
    Hike the Alum Cave Trail One of the park's most rewarding trails — about 4.4 miles round trip — passing through mossy forest, dramatic bluff formations, and out to sweeping mountain views. It's not too strenuous, it's genuinely beautiful the whole way, and there's something about completing a hike together in the first days of a marriage that feels exactly right.
  3. 03
    Take Your Time Through Cades Cove This 11-mile scenic loop through an open valley is one of the most visited places in the park for good reason. Pull over often. Watch the deer move through the morning mist. Explore the historic homesteads and old churches that have been here since the 1800s. On Wednesday and Saturday mornings it's vehicle-free until 10 AM — bikes and walkers only. Special.
  4. 04
    Stroll Downtown Gatlinburg Hand-pulled taffy, locally made fudge, craft shops stacked with mountain-made goods, and the kind of kitsch that somehow becomes deeply charming after dark when the lights come on. Walk the full strip together, stop for anything that catches your eye, and get the fudge. You'll regret it if you don't.
  5. 05
    Raft the Pigeon River The Upper Pigeon offers fast, fun, Class III-IV rapids and the Lower Pigeon is more relaxed — both are guided, both are safe, and both are a blast. There's something about navigating whitewater together early in a marriage that makes for an excellent metaphor and an even better memory. Several reputable outfitters operate right off the highway.
  6. 06
    Horseback Ride Through the Forest Multiple stables in the area offer guided trail rides through the national park, along creekbeds, and through stands of old hardwoods. You don't need experience — the horses know the trails better than anyone. It's peaceful, it's unhurried, and it's the kind of activity that creates an easy rhythm for conversation. A perfect afternoon.
  7. 07
    Hike to Laurel Falls The most visited waterfall in the park — and there's a reason for that. The trail is paved and relatively easy at 2.6 miles round trip, which makes it accessible no matter your fitness level. The falls themselves are genuinely stunning, cascading in two tiers through old-growth hemlock forest. Go early in the morning to beat the crowds.
  8. 08
    Drive the Roaring Fork Motor Nature Trail This narrow, winding one-way road winds through some of the most dramatic and untouched scenery in the entire park — rushing mountain streams, old-growth forest, mossy boulders, and hidden historic cabins still standing from the 1800s. It's closed in winter but worth every twist and turn when open. Slow down. Roll the windows down.
  9. 09
    Stargaze from Above the Treeline Great Smoky Mountains National Park is a certified International Dark Sky Park — meaning on a clear night, the Milky Way is visible with the naked eye. Drive up to a high elevation pull-off after dark, bring a blanket, and give yourselves at least 20 minutes to let your eyes adjust. It has a way of making everything feel very small and very good at the same time.
  10. 10
    Spend an Evening at Dollywood Yes, really. We mean it. Dollywood is genuinely one of the best theme parks in the country — world-class coasters, incredible live music, craft demonstrations, and a quality that most parks simply don't bother with. After dark, the whole park lights up in a way that's quietly magical. Go at least once. You'll understand immediately why we recommended it.

The Smokies reward the people who slow down enough to actually be in them. As newlyweds, you've just made the most important promise of your lives — give yourselves the space to let that settle. These mountains are very good at helping with that.

From Zach & Bridgett — We hope you love it up here as much as we do.
